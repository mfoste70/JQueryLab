Morning

As the first piece I composed that got actual traction, "Morning" was the initial spark I needed to pursue music performance and writing to the extent that I have.

This piece takes the form of four small excerpts to represent and illustrate the passage of time.

We're Going to War!

Strangely enough, this piece was born from a late-night party with some of my old friends. We all made this horrid concoction of like 6 Monster energy drinks a bunch of sour candy, it was vile but effective at fueling our "last hurrah" before going off to college.

For this piece, my goal was to practice blending instruments beyond their typical groupings while still giving each section its own identity/theme.

Seafarer

This piece started off as my attempt at putting my old clarinet to use. Surely if I wrote a piece with the clarinet at the forefront, I could one day learn to play it again, right? Unfortunately, I got carried away with all the harmonies and drama. But hey, I think it turned out alright.

Rococo

While more of an excerpt than a piece, this musical idea comes from a creative collaboration I was in with a few other students on campus where I was tasked to make something opulent, snobby, and grandiose, hence the title "Rococo".

Perhaps one day I'll build this into a proper piece. One day.
